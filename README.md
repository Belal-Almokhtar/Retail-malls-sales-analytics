# 🛍️ California Mall Customer Sales Analysis

> **End-to-end data analytics project** analyzing sales performance, customer behavior, and mall characteristics across 10 California shopping malls using **SQL** for data processing and **Power BI** for interactive visualization.

---

## 📌 Project Overview

This project simulates a real-world retail analytics scenario where a iam as  a data analyst is tasked with helping **mall operators, retailers, and marketing teams** make data-driven decisions. The analysis spans **99,458 transactions** across 10 California malls, covering the period **2021–2023**.

By joining three datasets — Sales, Customer Demographics, and Mall Characteristics — this project uncovers patterns in revenue performance, customer spending behavior, and the impact of mall attributes on sales outcomes.

---

## 🎯 Business Objectives

- Identify which malls and California cities drive the highest revenue
- Understand how customer demographics (age, gender) influence spending behavior
- Analyze how mall characteristics (size, age, store count) correlate with sales performance
- Detect seasonal and monthly sales trends to support planning and promotions
- Determine which product categories dominate revenue and where
- Provide actionable recommendations for mall operators and retail teams

---

## ❓ Business Questions

### Sales Performance
- **A1.** What is the total revenue per shopping mall?
- **A2.** Which 3 product categories generate the highest revenue, and what is their % share of total sales?
- **A3.** How does revenue change month by month and seasonally?

### Customer Behavior
- **B1.** What is the total revenue generated by each age group?
- **B2.** How does total spending differ between male and female customers?
- **B3.** What is the most preferred payment method per gender and age group?
- **B4.** How do customer demographics impact spending across different mall locations?

### Mall Characteristics
- **C1.** Is there a relationship between mall size (area_sqm), store count, and total revenue?
- **C2.** Which California city generates the highest retail revenue?

---

## 🔑 Key Findings

### Sales Performance
- **Del Amo Fashion Center** (Torrance) leads with **$50.87M** in total revenue, closely followed by **South Coast Plaza** (Costa Mesa) at **$50.56M**
- **Clothing** dominates with **45.33%** of total revenue ($114M), followed by Shoes at **26.46%** and Technology at **23.01%** — together accounting for ~95% of all sales
- Revenue trends show relatively **stable monthly performance** (~$9–10M/month), suggesting consistent demand with no extreme seasonality

### Customer Behavior
- **Female customers** account for approximately **60%** of total revenue vs. 40% for male customers
- The **30–45 age group** is the highest-spending segment at **$73.16M**, followed closely by the **45–60** group at **$71.81M**
- Younger customers (15–30) generate **$58.03M**, while seniors (60–75) contribute **$48.23M**

### Mall Characteristics
- **Los Angeles** leads all cities with **$62.01M** in revenue across its 3 malls (The Grove, Westfield Century City, Beverly Center)
- **Torrance** ($50.87M) and **Costa Mesa** ($50.56M) follow as the next strongest markets
- Mall size and store count show a **positive pattern** with revenue — larger, more established malls tend to outperform smaller ones

---

## 💡 Recommendations

1. **Double down on Clothing & Shoes** — with ~72% of revenue between them, retailers should prioritize these categories in prime mall locations
2. **Target the 30–60 age demographic** — this group drives the majority of spending and should be the focus of loyalty programs and promotions
3. **Focus marketing resources on Los Angeles, Torrance, and Costa Mesa** — these three markets account for over 60% of total revenue
4. **Investigate underperforming malls** — Fashion Valley, Irvine Spectrum, Glendale Galleria, The Grove, and Beverly Center all generate under $13M; a deep-dive analysis is recommended
5. **Optimize payment options** — understanding payment preferences by demographic can reduce checkout friction and increase conversion
